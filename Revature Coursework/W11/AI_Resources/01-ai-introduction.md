## The Concept

### What is Artificial Intelligence?

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is a branch of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include:

- **Learning** from data and experience
- **Reasoning** through problems
- **Perception** of the environment (vision, speech)
- **Natural language understanding** and generation
- **Decision-making** under uncertainty

At its core, AI involves creating algorithms and models that can process information, identify patterns, and make predictions or decisions based on that information.

### Types of AI

AI can be categorized in several ways:

#### By Capability

1. **Narrow AI (Weak AI)**
   - Designed for specific tasks
   - What we have today
   - Examples: Siri, recommendation systems, image recognition

2. **General AI (Strong AI)**
   - Human-level intelligence across all domains
   - Does not exist yet
   - Theoretical goal of AI research

3. **Superintelligent AI**
   - Surpasses human intelligence
   - Purely theoretical
   - Subject of philosophical and ethical debate

#### By Approach

1. **Rule-Based Systems**
   - Explicit programming of rules
   - "If X, then Y" logic
   - Limited adaptability

2. **Machine Learning**
   - Systems learn from data
   - Improve with experience
   - Statistical pattern recognition

3. **Deep Learning**
   - Neural networks with many layers
   - Excels at complex pattern recognition
   - Powers modern AI breakthroughs

### Current State of AI

Today's AI excels at:

- **Pattern Recognition**: Images, speech, text
- **Prediction**: Weather, stock prices, user behavior
- **Generation**: Text, images, code, music
- **Optimization**: Resource allocation, scheduling

Today's AI struggles with:

- True understanding and reasoning
- Common sense knowledge
- Transfer learning across domains
- Explaining its decisions (interpretability)

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Algorithm** | A set of rules or instructions for solving problems |
| **Model** | A trained AI system that can make predictions |
| **Training** | The process of teaching an AI using data |
| **Inference** | Using a trained model to make predictions |
| **Dataset** | Collection of data used for training |

## Summary

- AI refers to systems that can perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence
- We currently have Narrow AI; General AI remains theoretical
- AI approaches range from rule-based systems to deep learning
- Modern AI excels at pattern recognition, prediction, and generation
- AI is increasingly integrated into software development workflows
